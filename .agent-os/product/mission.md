# Mission

**Universal Mail** is an AI-first email client that treats your inbox as something to *understand*, not just *store*.

## The problem

People who run their lives in email juggle 2–4 accounts (work Gmail, personal Gmail, Outlook, an old Yahoo for receipts). Today they use:

- 3 different mobile apps, or
- A heavy desktop client that doesn't ship a real PWA, or
- Gmail Web pretending the other accounts don't exist.

And none of those clients **read the mail for you**. They show you a list. You still have to triage every message yourself.

## The product

One unified inbox. Three things the email itself does for you:

1. **Summary** — every thread, on open. One line + bullets + action items. No more skimming six-message threads to find the one ask.
2. **Reply drafts** — three tones (concise / friendly / formal), generated from the actual thread context, editable before send.
3. **Priority** — the inbox sorts itself. Urgent (real deadline, billing, security) floats up. Newsletters sink.

Everything mobile-first. PWA. Installable. Works offline for reading.

## Non-goals

- No contacts, no calendar, no tasks, no notes. Scope discipline beats feature sprawl.
- No "Sent later" or scheduling. v1.
- No native mobile app. PWA is enough.
- No AI features that act without confirmation. Every send is user-initiated.

## Who it's for

The "power inbox user" — founders, EAs, individual contributors managing real correspondence — who already accept that an AI assistant looking at their mail is a net positive **if** the privacy story is clean and the AI is fast enough to actually use.
